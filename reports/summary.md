## Summary — Customer Churn Descriptive Analytics
## 1. Objective

The goal of this project is to analyze customer churn patterns and identify key drivers using descriptive analytics. The analysis helps the company understand why customers leave, which customer groups are most at risk, and what actions can reduce churn.

## 2. Key Insights

## Overall Churn Rate
~26.5% of customers have churned.
Majority (73.5%) remain active, but churn is still financially impactful.

## Contract Type
Month-to-month customers churn the most.
One-year and two-year contracts have significantly lower churn, proving that long-term commitment reduces churn.

## Internet Service Type
Fiber optic customers show the highest churn.
Indicates dissatisfaction with service quality or price.

## Payment Method
Electronic check users show very high churn.
Automatic payment methods (credit card, bank transfer) show lower churn, possibly due to convenience.

## Tenure Cohort
Highest churn occurs in:
0–6 months
7–12 months
Customers who stay longer (24+ months) churn far less.
Indicates critical retention window is first 12 months.

## Monthly Charges
Customers with higher monthly charges show higher churn.
Suggests a need to review pricing strategy or offer loyalty discounts.

## Churn Reasons Breakdown
Top churn reasons include:
Competitor offerings
Price dissatisfaction
Support issues
Product quality concerns
These insights directly highlight operational issues.


## 3. Business Recommendations
Introduce retention offers for month-to-month customers.
Improve Fiber Optic service quality or pricing.
Incentivize auto-payment to reduce manual churn.
Focus on customer onboarding in first 6–12 months.
Create targeted offers for high-charge customers.


## 4. Conclusion

This churn analysis successfully identifies:
- High-risk customer segments
- Key churn drivers
- Actionable strategies to reduce churn

It lays a strong foundation for building a future churn prediction model (machine learning).
